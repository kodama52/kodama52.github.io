AccuWeather Inc. is an American media company that provides commercial weather forecasting services worldwide.



Key Objectives

1. Craft a comprehensive user experience for the AccuWeather Now video feature seamlessly integrated into the mobile application.

2. Enhance the user experience of the Health & Activities web page by implementing improvements to the design and functionality.

3. Develop a set of UX templates and standards to be utilized by the UX team in upcoming projects, promoting consistency and efficiency in the design process.


Summary

As a UX Designer at AccuWeather, I had the privilege of working on several significant projects for both the mobile application and desktop website, catering to a vast consumer base of 1.5 billion people. Among these, two stand-out projects that I contributed to were the AccuWeather Now video feature and the Health & Activities page. Furthermore, I collaborated closely with the UX and UI teams to refine and enhance the existing design system, ensuring a seamless user experience across all touchpoints.

Understanding the Service


AccuWeather Now is a streaming platform that features 24 hours of continuous daily programming dedicated to all things weather.

Above Average Design Standards


My objective was to design a holistic user experience for the AccuWeather Now feature on the mobile application.

To ensure a seamless and enjoyable experience, I undertook a thorough analysis of seven video-enabled competitor services, examining their revenue models and features.

All seven companies have robust live video integration capabilities. By juxtaposing their strengths, I was able to identify the features that would most benefit AccuWeather.






Category 1 Designs


Initially, I developed a design concept for a fly-out player. I deemed this to be a crucial feature as it would enable users to effortlessly navigate weather-related content on the application while simultaneously viewing the video. The "AccuWeather Now" labeled bottom-floating bar would house the video player, which would expand into a hovering live video player. The player would include various functionalities such as pause, mute, minimize, resize, and relocation, giving users complete control over their viewing experience.

Benefits of Utilizing The Health & Activities Page


AccuWeather's Health & Activities feature is available on both mobile and desktop sites, providing a diverse range of activities and health conditions that are impacted by the current weather. This feature aims to help users make informed decisions about their daily activities and well-being by providing valuable insights into how the weather might affect their health and plans. Whether it's allergies, exercise, or other outdoor activities, the Health & Activities feature provides users with relevant and timely information, allowing them to stay prepared and optimize their lifestyle according to the current weather conditions.


Learning from the Competition


As a UX designer at AccuWeather, I took on the project of revamping the Health & Activities page with the goal of improving the user experience. In my analysis of the existing page, I identified several areas that could benefit from enhancements.

To create an optimal user experience, I conducted a thorough analysis of competitors in the weather and health services industry.

With insights gained from my research, I narrowed my focus to three key areas that would help to create a more intuitive and user-friendly Health & Activities page. My aim was to provide users with valuable and relevant information in a more streamlined and organized way, making it easier for them to make informed decisions about their health and activities based on the weather forecast.



(1). Exploring Current Features


During my review of the tiles on the Health & Activities page, I observed that different variables were being measured using different scales - some using a low-to-extreme scale while others used a poor-to-ideal scale. This inconsistency can lead to confusion for users. In order to address this issue, I began to develop tiles that display intensity on a single, consistent scale.


To enhance the quality of the tiles, I re-designed them with a uniform intensity scale, rather than the previous two opposing scales. In order to label the AccuWeather tiles appropriately, I conducted extensive research by cross-referencing a range of relevant applications, including allergy, health, fitness, and fishing.

(2).Organizitation of H&A


To optimize the use of the tiles, I developed a new method for presenting important information for each unique user interaction.


Explorations


Through my explorations, I arrived at the design displayed below, which allows users to create an AccuWeather account to save their preferences and receive tailored information based on their location. The "Curated For You" section features six tiles that can be removed or replaced according to the user's preferences.


Variation of the Graphs


The graphs on the Health & Activities page were unclear and difficult to interpret, as they only displayed one variable while the tile description discussed multiple variables. Additionally, below the graph, there were more variables listed that had no connection to the graph. This lack of cohesion and clarity made it confusing for users to understand the information presented.
